The person behind the pins

You come for the fit.
You come back for Ms. Kim.

Kim A Tailor isn't a chain and doesn't pretend to be. It's one skilled owner at one workbench on East Main, doing careful work and treating everyone who walks in like a neighbor — which, downtown, most of them are.

i.

A quiet reputation, earned

Ask around downtown Richardson about alterations and one name keeps coming up. Ms. Kim has built a following the honest way: careful stitch by careful stitch, one happy customer telling the next. The reviews read like a chorus — kind, welcoming, always smiling, and quick.

People notice the little things. She listens closely when you describe how you want something to fit, understands quickly, and offers good advice instead of just nodding along. Regulars mention the small warmth of the place too — a bottle of water, a piece of chocolate, a genuine hello.

And then there's the speed. It's the thing reviewers can't stop mentioning: turnaround so fast it feels unbelievable, rush fixes in half an hour, same-day work when you're in a bind. That combination — real care and real speed — is why customers stay for a decade and more.
