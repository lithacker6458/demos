03

Built for this climate

Concrete at 5,800 feet is a different sport.

Cedar City sits near 5,800 feet, where concrete freezes at night and bakes by afternoon for months at a stretch. Freeze–thaw cycles destroy shortcuts: bad base prep, missing control joints, and rushed finishing all show up by the second winter. That's why our standard is the boring stuff done right — compacted base, correct joints, proper cure — on every pour, from a patio to a curb line.

Base & subgrade first

A slab is only as good as what's under it. Grading and compaction come before any concrete truck shows up.

Joints where they belong

Control joints placed for the slab, not for convenience — so cracking happens where it's planned, not where it's ugly.

Finished for the freeze

Finish and cure matched to high-desert conditions, so surfaces hold up through the freeze–thaw seasons Cedar City actually gets.
