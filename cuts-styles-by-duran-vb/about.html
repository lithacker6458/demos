The family behind the chair

Cuts & Styles by Duran isn't a franchise or a chain that rotates through faces. It's the Mendez family — and it has been since 1994.

01

The Mendez family

Marcial Mendez — the “Duran” on the sign — opened the shop on Main Street in 1994 as a Master Barber. Three decades later he's still there, now working alongside his son Abner and a small team of stylists. Between them the family carries four generations of barbering, and it shows in the details: the exact-to-request cut, the clean line-up, the hot straight-razor finish.

The proof is in who keeps coming back. Customers who first sat in these chairs twenty years ago still book with the same barbers — some now bringing their own kids and grandkids. That kind of loyalty isn't bought with a coupon. It's earned one honest cut at a time.

And because a barbershop should serve the whole household, we grew into a full salon too — color, highlights, perms, and women's styling right alongside the classic barber work. Same room, same standard, same family.
