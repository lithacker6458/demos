One Flap Turned Into Two

“Back in 1999, I put up a little two-flap fireworks stand on the corner. It wasn’t big or fancy – just a retirement hobby to keep me busy and give folks a place to get great fireworks at a fair price. But one flap turned into two, two turned into four… and before long, that little hobby started turning into something real.” – Benny (and Shari & Sam)

1999

A two-flap corner stand

Benny puts up a little wooden fireworks stand as a retirement hobby – a place for folks to get great fireworks at a fair price. Shari’s there from the early days, helping customers, picking products, and keeping the whole thing running smoother than Benny ever could’ve alone.

2008

Sam joins the family

“Hard-working kid, sharp as a tack, and he fit right in from day one.” Now the three of them travel to demos to hand-select fireworks, living by one rule: we never buy anything we wouldn’t shoot off ourselves.

“The termites finally decided they wanted it more than we did!” So they did what Texans do – they rebuilt. When the new, massive six-flap stand went up, it got a name they were proud to put out front: Benny’s Bad Azz Fireworks.
