Since 1996

A living-room dream that became Fort Worth's barbershop.

Rudy Avitia has always had a passion for cutting hair. Inspired by his mother, a retired cosmetologist, Rudy started cutting in his living room at just 13. That passion became Rudy the Barber in 1996, and 30 years later it is still Fort Worth's premier old-school shop.

"We do old-school barbering for men who take pride in how they look. I'm a true-born barber, and in my shop, I don't accept anything less than perfection." Rudy Avitia, Owner & Founder

The shop spent roughly a decade on West Magnolia Avenue before rising rents pushed it to Hemphill Street. That fight turned Rudy into a community advocate: he and his brother Ricardo founded "Hemphill No Se Vende," working to preserve the neighborhood's character. "We do want development, but the right type of development."
