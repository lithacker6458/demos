What We Place

The Full Reinforcing Scope.

Rebar, post-tension, and detailing are different disciplines — XEGR runs all of them so your reinforcing package answers to one accountable subcontractor. Here's exactly what that covers.

[ XEGR jobsite photo — rebar placement ]

01 — Rebar Placement & Tying

Reinforcing steel, set to the drawings.

The core of the trade: taking reinforcing steel from delivery to a placed, tied, inspection-ready mat. We handle footings, grade beams, walls, columns, and elevated decks — placed for correct spacing, cover, and lap, and supported so it holds through the pour.

  • ▮Cutting, bending, and field fabrication of reinforcing steel
  • ▮Placement & tying of mats, walls, columns, and footings
  • ▮Splices, dowels, chairs, bolsters, and support systems
  • ▮Spacing, cover, and lap verified before the pour
[ XEGR jobsite photo — PT cable pull ]

02 — Post-Tension (PT Cable) Operations

Tensioned slabs and decks, done in-house.

Post-tensioning is where reinforcing gets specialized. We run PT operations end to end — laying out and chairing tendons, pulling cable, and coordinating stressing and finishing so tensioned members reach their designed strength.

  • ▮Tendon layout, profiling, and chairing to the PT drawings
  • ▮Cable pulling and placement for slabs and elevated decks
  • ▮Stressing coordination and elongation documentation
  • ▮Pocket finishing and close-out with the pour schedule
[ XEGR — placing drawings / detailing ]

03 — Reinforcing Steel Detailing

Design turned into a buildable field package.

Good placement starts before anyone's on site. Our detailing coordination translates the structural design into accurate placing drawings, bar lists, and bend schedules — so what reaches the field is clear, complete, and ready to build.

  • ▮Shop- and placing-drawing coordination
  • ▮Bar lists and bend schedules for fabrication
  • ▮Placement plans the field crew can build straight from
  • ▮RFI-ready coordination with the structural set
[ XEGR jobsite photo — structural concrete ]

04 — Structural & Exterior Concrete

The reinforced systems, and the site around them.

Reinforcing exists to make concrete structural — and XEGR carries that through the reinforced-concrete systems themselves plus exterior concrete improvements that finish a site cleanly.

  • ▮Reinforced structural concrete systems
  • ▮Slab-on-grade reinforcing and placement
  • ▮Exterior & site concrete improvements
  • ▮Coordination with formwork and finishing crews
[ XEGR jobsite photo — field layout ]

05 — Field Coordination & Layout

The part that keeps your schedule intact.

A reinforcing crew is only as good as how it fits the wider job. We coordinate layout with your superintendent and the trades around us, sequencing our work so the reinforcing is ready when the pour is — not the reason it waits.

  • ▮On-site layout tied to control lines and structural grid
  • ▮Sequencing around formwork, MEP, and embeds
  • ▮Daily coordination with the GC's superintendent
  • ▮Pour-readiness held to the master schedule
