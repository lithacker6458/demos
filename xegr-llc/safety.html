Safety & Quality

Safety Is The Assembly.

A safe, efficient, and precise process isn't a policy line — it's how we place every bar and stress every cable. This is the page a project manager should open first, so here's exactly how XEGR runs a safe reinforcing job.

Our Commitment

Nobody gets hurt placing our steel.

General contractors judge a subcontractor on safety before price, before schedule, before anything. We build to that reality: every crew works to the GC's site rules and our own standard, and reinforcing is placed to be inspected — not to be reworked.

Fully insured — general liability & workers' compensation. Certificates of insurance and additional-insured endorsements are provided on request.

The commitments below are how we operate, framed honestly as process. Formal safety-program documentation and our current EMR are available to any GC on request.

Jobsite Safety Discipline

How we keep the site safe.

▮

PPE & site rules

Every crew works in required PPE and follows the general contractor's site-specific safety plan, orientation, and access rules — no exceptions, every shift.

▮

Housekeeping & hazard awareness

Reinforcing work creates trip, puncture, and impalement hazards. We keep work areas clean, cap exposed bar, and stay aware of the trades and equipment around us.

▮

Toolbox talks & culture

Regular toolbox talks and a crew culture where anyone can stop work over a hazard. Safety is a habit we bring onto your site, not a binder we point at.

Quality & Inspection

Placed right, and ready to inspect.

Quality on a reinforcing job is measurable: it's whether the steel matches the drawings before it disappears under concrete. We build every placement to pass inspection the first time.

Tie & placement inspection

Before anything's covered, reinforcing is checked against the structural drawings — bar size, spacing, cover, laps, and supports all verified prior to pour.

Spec-conformance QA

Placement built to the structural drawings and project specifications, so third-party and GC inspections are a confirmation — not a punch list.

PT stressing records

On post-tension work, elongation and stressing are documented so tensioned members are verified to the PT design and ready for sign-off.

Documentation

Everything a GC needs to prequalify us.

We're set up to answer a prequalification package quickly. We carry general liability & workers' comp; formal safety program details, EMR, insurance certificates, and references are provided on request — just reach out and we'll turn them around.
